Map Overview

  1. View 1: The Yunnan Region of China. The Yunnan Province is a mountainous region in Southwestern China. The Province borders Myanmar, Vietnam, and Laos.
  2. View 2: Yunnan's Plateau Region. The region is home to many lakes, comprising a total surface area of 1,066 km2. Nine of these "plateau" lakes are recognized as having surface areas over 30 km2. The lakes span multiple watersheds. Fuxian Lake, Xingyun Lake, and Qilu Lake are all in the same watershed.
  3. View 3: The Yuxi City District. Three of these "plateau lakes" (Fuxian, Xingyun, and Qilu Lakes) fall entirely within the Yuxi City District of Yunnan. The Yuxi City government is responsible for the restoration and protection of these lakes.
  4. View 4: Three Yuxi Lakes. Of these three lakes, Fuxian is substantially larger and deeper than Qilu or Xingyun. From a satellite image, Qilu and Xingyun appear green while Fuxian is dark blue due to being deeper and cleaner.
  5. View 5: Comparing lakes of the Yuxi with Lake Erie. Lake Erie faces similar threats to water quality despite its larger volume and surface area. Lake Erie is perhaps 100 times bigger than the other lakes, although Fuxian lake is deeper on average than Lake Erie.
